Tribute to Nikola Tesla

The Father of the modern era

Nikola Tesla was an Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, physicist and futurist who is best known for developing the Alternate current (AC) induction motor, which revolutionised the way electricity was used, since the AC was easy to elevate to high voltages, making transportation a lot cheaper and easier. Later he developed the polyphasic alternatic current, know nowadays as the triphasic current, used in the entire world.

Timeline of his life

  • 1856: Born into a Serb family in the Austrian Empire.
  • 1870: Moved to Karlovac to attend school.
  • 1873: Graduated from school and returned to his hometown.
  • 1881: Moved to Budapest to work for the Budapest Telephone Exchange.
  • 1882: Moved to Paris and began working with the Continental Edison Company.
  • 1884: Moved to the United States and continued working for Edison's Company
  • 1885: Founded his own company, Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing
  • 1887: Developed an induction motor running on alternating current which rapidly expanded in the US and Europe
  • 1889: Starded developing the Tesla Coil
  • 1893: The famous polyphase AC system is born. Now known as the triphasic current used all around the world
  • 1943: Tesla died alone in Room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel

    • About his inventions

    Induction motor
    An induction motor or 3 phase induction motor is an AC electric motor in which the electric current in the rotor needed to produce torque is obtained by electromagnetic induction from the magnetic field of the stator winding.An induction motor can therefore be made without electrical connections to the rotor. An induction motor's rotor can be either wound type or squirrel-cage type.
    The Tesla coil
    The Tesla coil is an electrical resonant transformer circuit.It is used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high frequency alternating-current electricity. Tesla experimented with a number of different configurations consisting of two, or sometimes three, coupled resonant electric circuits.
    Three-phase electric power
    hree-phase electric power is a common method of alternating current electric power generation, transmission, and distribution.It is a type of polyphase system and is the most common method used by electrical grids worldwide to transfer power. It is also used to power large motors and other heavy loads.
